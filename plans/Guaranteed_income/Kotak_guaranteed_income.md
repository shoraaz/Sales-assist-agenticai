## 4. Plan Variants Breakdown

You must choose one of three plan options at inception[cite: 986].

### Plan Option A: Early Income
* **Benefit:** Receive regular Income Benefits starting from the end of the 1st policy month or 1st policy year, as chosen[cite: 991].
* **Income Benefit:** Comprises a **Guaranteed Income** and **Cash Bonuses** (if declared)[cite: 992].
* **Guaranteed Income:** Calculated as 0.70% p.a. of the Sum Assured on Maturity[cite: 998].
* **Payout:** Monthly income is calculated as 96% * (yearly income benefit payout) / 12[cite: 994].

### Plan Option B: Paid-Up Additions
* **Benefit:** The Income Benefits (Guaranteed Income and Cash Bonuses, if declared) are used to purchase additional Sum Assured in the form of **Paid-Up Additions** (PUA) throughout the policy term[cite: 1000, 1006].
* **Cash Bonus:** Cash Bonuses (if declared) on the Sum Assured on Maturity and on the PUA are also utilized to purchase more PUA[cite: 1001].
* **Guaranteed Income:** Calculated as 0.70% p.a. of the Sum Assured on Maturity[cite: 1006, 1007].
* **Payout:** The accrued PUA are payable on Death or Maturity[cite: 1004].
* **Flexibility:** You have the option to encash the Cash Value of the accrued PUA[cite: 1004].

### Plan Option C: Premium Saver
* **Benefit:** Cash bonuses (if declared) are payable starting from the end of the premium payment term until the end of the policy term[cite: 1014].
* **Payout:** Cash Bonuses are paid monthly or yearly, as opted[cite: 1015].
* **Guaranteed Loyalty Additions (GLAs):**
    * The plan pays GLAs equal to 110% of the Annualised premium during the last two years of the PPT[cite: 1016].
    * Payable at the beginning of the year[cite: 1017].
    * **For PPT 8:** Paid at the start of years 7 & 8[cite: 1023].
    * **For PPT 10:** Paid at the start of years 9 & 10[cite: 1023].
    * **For PPT 12:** Paid at the start of years 11 & 12[cite: 1023].
    * These GLAs can be used to pay your due premiums[cite: 1018, 1024].

### C. Bonus Structure
Bonuses are not guaranteed and depend on the performance of the participating fund[cite: 1057].

* **Cash Bonus:**
    * The company may declare Cash Bonus Rates annually, expressed as a percentage of the Sum Assured on Maturity and any Paid-Up Additions[cite: 1061, 1071].
    * Payable monthly or yearly as opted[cite: 1063].
    * Under Plan B, these are used to purchase Paid-Up Additions[cite: 1076].
* **Paid-Up Additions (PUA):**
    * Under Plan B, declared Cash Bonuses are used to purchase PUA[cite: 1076].
    * These PUAs also participate in future profits[cite: 1078].
* **Interim Bonus:**
    * May be payable in the event of a death or surrender claim that occurs between policy anniversaries[cite: 1081, 1082].
    * Calculated proportionately for the period from the previous policy anniversary to the date of exit[cite: 1084].
* **Terminal Bonus:**
    * May be paid upon exit of the policy (due to death, surrender, or maturity)[cite: 1088].
    * May be declared for policies exiting due to death if the policy has completed 25 policy years[cite: 1091, 1092].
    * May be declared for policies exiting due to surrender if the policy has completed three policy years[cite: 1091, 1093].

## 7. Policy Administration

* **Grace Period:**
    * **30 days** for yearly, half-yearly, and quarterly modes[cite: 1314].
    * **15 days** for the monthly mode[cite: 1314].
    * The policy remains in-force during this period[cite: 1315].
* **Lapse:**
    * If premiums are discontinued during the first policy year, the policy will lapse after the grace period, and no benefits are payable[cite: 1317].
* **Revival:**
    * A lapsed or Reduced Paid-Up policy can be revived within **five policy years** from the due date of the first unpaid premium[cite: 1324].
    * Revival requires payment of outstanding premiums with interest (currently 9% p.a.)[cite: 1328, 1329].
    * Evidence of good health may be required if revival is after 6 months from the first unpaid premium[cite: 1328, 1329].
    * Upon revival, all benefits are reinstated[cite: 1332].
* **Surrender:**
    * **Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV):** Acquired after full premiums for at least 2 consecutive policy years have been paid[cite: 1346].
    * **Special Surrender Value (SSV):** Acquired after the first policy year, provided premiums for at least 1 policy year have been paid[cite: 1347, 1385].
    * The Surrender Value payable is the **higher of GSV or SSV**[cite: 1348, 1387].
* **Reduced Paid-Up (RPU):**
    * If a policy has acquired surrender value and premiums are discontinued, it converts to an RPU policy by default[cite: 1394].
    * Once RPU, the policy is not eligible for future Cash Bonuses[cite: 1395].
    * The Sum Assured on Maturity, Sum Assured on Death, Guaranteed Income (Plan A & B), and Guaranteed Loyalty Additions (Plan C) are all reduced proportionately[cite: 1397, 1398, 1410, 1413, 1415].
* **Policy Loans:**
    * A loan can be taken for a minimum of ₹10,000[cite: 1455].
    * The maximum loan amount is up to 50% of the policy's Surrender Value[cite: 1455].
    * The policy must be assigned to the company as security[cite: 1456].
    * The interest rate (currently 9.23671% p.a. compounded half-yearly) is specified by the company[cite: 1460].

## 8. Key Terms & Exclusions

* **Free Look Period:**
    * The Policyholder has **30 days** from the date of receiving the policy document to review the terms and conditions[cite: 1440].
    * If not agreeable, the policy can be returned for cancellation and a refund of premium, less deductions for proportionate risk premium, stamp duty, and medical expenses[cite: 1441, 1442, 1443].
* **Suicide Exclusion:**
    * If the Life Insured commits suicide within **12 months** from the date of commencement of risk, 80% of the Total Premiums paid will be payable[cite: 1450].
    * If suicide occurs within 12 months of revival (when revival is done *after* 6 months from the first unpaid premium), the benefit payable is the higher of 80% of Total Premiums Paid or the Surrender Value[cite: 1453].
* **Definitions:**
    * **Annualised Premium:** The premium payable in a policy year, excluding taxes, rider premiums, underwriting extra premiums, and modal loadings[cite: 1049, 1286].
    * **Total Premiums Paid:** Includes extra premiums but excludes GST and Rider Premiums[cite: 1369].
