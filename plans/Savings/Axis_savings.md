## Key Features & Benefits

* **Choice of Terms:** Flexibility to choose from various Premium Payment Terms and Policy Terms[cite: 22, 23].
* **Guaranteed Additions:** Provides 5.5% of the Guaranteed Sum Assured on Maturity as Guaranteed Additions at the end of each of the first five policy years[cite: 26].
* **Death Benefit:**
    * On or before 10 policy years: Guaranteed Death Benefit + accrued Guaranteed Additions + Paid Up Additions (if any) + Terminal Bonus (if any)[cite: 28].
    * After 10 policy years: 110% of Guaranteed Death Benefit + accrued Guaranteed Additions + Paid Up Additions (if any) + Terminal Bonus (if any)[cite: 33].
* **Maturity Benefit:** 110% of Guaranteed Sum Assured on Maturity + accrued Guaranteed Additions + accrued Paid Up Additions (if any) + Terminal Bonus (if any)[cite: 35].
* **Settlement and Commutation Benefit:** Flexibility for the policyholder or nominee to convert the lump-sum maturity or death benefit into a regular income for 10 years[cite: 37]. The future incomes can be commuted into a lump sum at any time during the payout[cite: 49].
* **Tax Benefit:** Tax benefits may be available on premiums paid and benefits received as per prevailing tax laws[cite: 39].

## Benefits in Detail

### Maturity Benefit

If the life insured survives to the end of the policy term (and the policy is in force), the following is payable[cite: 96]:
* 110% of Guaranteed Sum Assured on Maturity [cite: 96]
* Accrued Guaranteed Additions [cite: 96]
* Accrued Paid Up Additions, if any [cite: 101]
* Terminal Bonus, if any [cite: 101]

### Death Benefit

* **For Single Pay:** A sum of:
    * Guaranteed Death Benefit [cite: 101]
    * Accrued Guaranteed Additions [cite: 101]
    * Accrued Paid Up Additions (if any) [cite: 101]
    * Terminal Bonus (if any) [cite: 101]
    * *Where Guaranteed Death Benefit (Single Pay) is the higher of: 125% of Single Premium, GSAM, or any absolute amount assured to be paid on death (110% of GSAM)*[cite: 101, 106].
* **For Limited & Regular Pay (Death on or before 10 policy years):** A sum of:
    * Guaranteed Death Benefit [cite: 106]
    * Accrued Guaranteed Additions [cite: 106]
    * Accrued Paid Up Additions (if any) [cite: 106]
    * Terminal Bonus (if any) [cite: 106]
* **For Limited & Regular Pay (Death after 10 policy years):** A sum of:
    * 110% x Guaranteed Death Benefit [cite: 106]
    * Accrued Guaranteed Additions [cite: 106]
    * Accrued Paid Up Additions (if any) [cite: 106]
    * Terminal Bonus (if any) [cite: 106]
    * *Where Guaranteed Death Benefit (Ltd/Reg Pay) is the higher of: 11 times the Annualised Premium, 105% of total premiums paid, GSAM, or any absolute amount assured to be paid on death (110% of GSAM)*[cite: 106].

### Terminal Illness Benefit

* If the Life Insured is diagnosed with a terminal illness (likely to lead to death within 6 months), the policyholder can request a payment[cite: 111].
* **Benefit:** Up to 50% of the Guaranteed Maturity Sum Assured (or RPU Sum Assured)[cite: 111].
* **Maximum:** Capped at ₹10 lakhs (cumulative) under all policies with this benefit from the Company[cite: 111].
* This benefit can be availed only once[cite: 111].
* The amount paid will be offset from the final policy proceeds (death, surrender, or maturity)[cite: 111].

## Bonus Details

* **Guaranteed Additions:** 5.5% of GSAM added at the end of each year for the first five policy years[cite: 26].
* **Annual Bonus (Paid Up Additions):**
    * Declared every year post completion of 2 Policy Years in the form of Paid Up Additions (PUA)[cite: 96].
    * Payable on surrender, death, or maturity[cite: 96].
    * **Bonus Utilization Options:**
        1.  **Paid Up Additions:** Bonus accrues and is paid in full on death or maturity. Surrender value of PUA is paid on surrender[cite: 96].
        2.  **Premium Offset:** Surrender value of PUA is used to offset future premiums. Any excess is paid to the policyholder[cite: 96].
        3.  **Paid In Cash:** Surrender value of PUA is paid in cash to the policyholder every year[cite: 96].
* **Terminal Bonus:**
    * An additional bonus paid only **once** on death, surrender, or maturity[cite: 96].
    * Payable only if the policy has been in force for at least five years (from the end of the 60th month onwards)[cite: 96].

## Premium Discontinuance / Surrender

### Surrender Value

* The policy acquires a Surrender Value (SV) which is the higher of Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV) or Special Surrender Value (SSV)[cite: 184, 185].
* **Acquiring GSV:**
    * **Single Pay:** Immediately on payment of single premium[cite: 188].
    * **Limited/Regular Pay:** On payment of two full years' premium[cite: 189].
* **Acquiring SSV:**
    * **Single Pay:** Immediately after receipt of single premium[cite: 205, 208].
    * **Limited/Regular Pay:** After completion of the first policy year, provided one full year's premium has been received[cite: 206, 207].

### Discontinuance of Premiums

* **Before Acquiring SV:** If premium is not paid by the end of the Grace Period, the policy shall **Lapse**[cite: 223]. Insurance cover stops, and no benefits are payable[cite: 224]. The policy can be revived within 5 years[cite: 224].
* **After Acquiring SV:** If premium is not paid by the end of the Grace Period, the policy becomes a **Reduced Paid Up (RPU)** policy[cite: 226].
    * Benefits (GSAM and Guaranteed Death Benefit) are reduced by an "RPU Factor"[cite: 229, 230].
    * RPU policies will not participate in future bonuses[cite: 247].
    * All rider benefits will cease[cite: 247].

## Key Terms & Conditions

### Free Look Period

* You have **30 days** from the date of receiving the policy document to review the terms and conditions[cite: 289].
* If you disagree, you can return the policy for cancellation and receive a refund of the premium paid, minus proportionate risk premium, medical exam costs (if any), and stamp duty charges[cite: 290, 291].

### Grace Period

* **Monthly Mode:** 15 days from the premium due date[cite: 216, 293].
* **All Other Modes (Annual, Semi-annual, Quarterly):** 30 days from the premium due date[cite: 216, 293].
* The insurance coverage continues during the grace period[cite: 217, 295].

### Exclusion (Suicide)

* If the Life Insured dies by suicide within 12 months from the date of inception or the date of revival, the policy terminates[cite: 297].
* The Company will pay:
    * Higher of the Surrender Value or (total premiums paid + underwriting extra premiums + modal loadings) if the policy has acquired SV[cite: 300].
    * (Total premiums paid + underwriting extra premiums + modal loadings) if the policy has not acquired SV[cite: 302].

### Policy Revival

* A **Lapsed** or **Reduced Paid Up (RPU)** policy can be revived within **five years** from the due date of the first unpaid premium[cite: 252, 268].
* Revival is subject to:
    * Payment of all overdue premiums with interest and/or late fees[cite: 253, 269].
    * Producing evidence of insurability acceptable to the company[cite: 261, 270].
