# HDFC Life Guaranteed Savings Plan
[cite: 3, 12]

**Plan Type:** An Individual Non-Linked Non-Participating Savings Life Insurance Plan [cite: 4, 13]

---

## Key Features & Benefits
[cite: 20]

* **Life Insurance Cover:** Provides a Sum Assured on Death. [cite: 21]
    * **Single Pay:** 1.25 times or 10 times the Single Premium (as chosen at inception). [cite: 21]
    * **Limited Pay:** 10 times the Annualised Premium. [cite: 21]
* **Guaranteed Maturity:** A guaranteed lump sum is paid at maturity, provided all due premiums have been paid. [cite: 22]
* **Premium Flexibility:** Option to pay once (Single Pay) or for a limited number of years (Limited Pay) at a frequency of your choice. [cite: 23]
* **Hassle-Free Issuance:** Policy is issued based on a self-declaration of good health (DOGH) [cite: 9], with no medical examination required. [cite: 24]
* **Tax Benefits:** May be available under Section 80C & 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, as per prevailing tax laws. [cite: 25]

## Benefits in Detail
[cite: 35]

### A. Maturity Benefit
[cite: 36]
On survival to the end of the Policy Term, the 'Sum Assured on Maturity' is paid as a lump sum, provided all due premiums have been paid. [cite: 37] The policy terminates upon payment. [cite: 38]

### B. Death Benefit
[cite: 39]
A **Waiting Period of 90 days** from the policy's inception applies to death benefits for reasons other than accidental death. [cite: 40]

* **Death During Waiting Period (First 90 days):**
    * **Accidental Death:** Sum Assured on Death is payable. [cite: 42]
    * **Death due to other causes:** 100% of Total Premiums paid will be refunded. [cite: 42]
* **Death After Waiting Period (After 90 days):**
    * **Any Cause:** Sum Assured on Death is payable. [cite: 43]

**"Sum Assured on Death"** is defined as the highest of: [cite: 42, 43]
1.  1.25 times or 10 times the Single Premium (for Single Pay) / 10 times the Annualised Premium (for Limited Pay).
2.  105% of Total Premiums paid.
3.  Sum Assured on Maturity.

The policy terminates upon payment of the Death Benefit. [cite: 49]

## What If Due Premiums Are Not Paid
[cite: 54]

### A. Lapsation
[cite: 55]
* If the policy has **not** acquired a Surrender Value and premiums are not paid past the Grace Period, the policy will lapse. [cite: 58]
* No benefits are payable under a lapsed policy. [cite: 59]
* A lapsed policy can be revived within 5 consecutive years from the date of the first unpaid premium. [cite: 60]

### B. Paid-up
[cite: 61]
* If the policy **has** acquired a Surrender Value and premiums are not paid past the Grace Period, the policy will become paid-up. [cite: 62]
* Benefits are reduced to a "Paid-up Sum Assured" (for both Maturity and Death), calculated based on the ratio of premiums paid to total premiums payable. [cite: 63, 64]
* The minimum death benefit for a paid-up policy will be 105% of total premiums paid. [cite: 70]

### C. Surrender
[cite: 71]
* The policyholder can surrender the policy during the policy term. [cite: 72]
* The Surrender Benefit will be the **higher** of the Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV) and the Special Surrender Value (SSV). [cite: 73]
* **Acquiring Surrender Value:**
    * **Single Pay:** Acquired upon payment of the Single Premium. [cite: 56, 75]
    * **Limited Pay:** Acquired after the first policy year, provided one full year's premium has been received. [cite: 57, 74]

## Other Features

### Liquidity Through Policy Loans
[cite: 91]
* Policy loans are available during the policy term. [cite: 92]
* **Maximum Loan Amount:** 80% of the Surrender Value. [cite: 94]
* **Interest Rate:** The current interest rate is 9.5% p.a. (This is reviewed half-yearly). [cite: 95]
* Any outstanding loan and interest will be deducted from benefits paid. [cite: 99]
* An in-force or fully paid-up policy will not be foreclosed for non-repayment of the loan. [cite: 100]

## Key Terms & Conditions
[cite: 107]

### A. Exclusions
[cite: 109]
* **Suicide:** If death due to suicide occurs within 12 months from the date of risk commencement or revival, the nominee will be entitled to at least 80% of total premiums paid or the surrender value, whichever is higher, provided the policy is in force. [cite: 111]
* **Waiting Period (Non-Accidental):** In case of death due to causes other than accident during the 90-day waiting period, total premiums paid shall be refunded. [cite: 113]
* **Accidental Death (Waiting Period Exclusions):** No benefit is paid for accidental death claims during the waiting period if caused by:
    * Influence of drugs, alcohol, or narcotics (unless prescribed). [cite: 117]
    * Intentional self-inflicted injuries or suicide attempts. [cite: 118]
    * Deliberate exposure to exceptional danger (except to save human life). [cite: 119]
    * Violation of the law or active participation in an act with criminal intent. [cite: 120]

### B. Grace Period
[cite: 121]
* **Single Premium:** Not applicable. [cite: 122]
* **Monthly Frequency:** 15 days. [cite: 122]
* **Quarterly/Half-yearly/Annual Frequency:** 30 days. [cite: 122]
* The policy remains in force with risk cover during the grace period. [cite: 123]

### C. Policy Revival
[cite: 125]
* A discontinued (lapsed) policy can be revived within **five years** from the due date of the first unpaid premium. [cite: 128]
* Revival is subject to receipt of all due premiums, interest (currently 9.5% p.a.), and company approval of insurability. [cite: 127, 128, 129]

### F. Cancellation in the Free-Look Period
[cite: 141]
* The policyholder has **30 days** from the date of receiving the policy to return it if they disagree with any terms. [cite: 142]
* The premium paid will be refunded, subject to deductions for: [cite: 144]
    * Proportionate risk premium for the period of cover.
    * Expenses incurred by the company on medical examination (if any).
    * Stamp duty charges.
