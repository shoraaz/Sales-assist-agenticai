## 2. Key Features

* **Guaranteed Income:** Provides a guaranteed regular income, expressed as a percentage of the Sum Assured (11%, 12%, or 13% p.a.), after the premium payment term[cite: 749, 777, 779].
* **Guaranteed Maturity Benefit:** A guaranteed lump sum, equal to the Sum Assured, is paid at the end of the policy term[cite: 751, 781].
* **Life Cover:** A full Guaranteed Death Benefit is provided for the entire policy term, even during the income payout period[cite: 725, 752, 801].
* **Payout Flexibility:** Offers the flexibility to choose the income payout period [cite: 729] and frequency (monthly or yearly)[cite: 774, 775].
* **Tax Benefits:** Policyholders may be eligible for tax benefits on premiums paid (under Section 80C and 80D for Critical Illness rider) and on policy proceeds (under Section 10(10D)) as per prevailing tax laws[cite: 730, 750, 751, 791, 792, 794, 795].
* **Optional Riders:** Provides the flexibility to enhance protection by adding optional riders[cite: 782, 783].

## 5. Benefit Details

### A. Survival Benefit (Guaranteed Income - GI)

* A regular, guaranteed income (GI) is paid as a survival benefit after the Premium Payment Term (PPT) is completed and continues for the rest of the policy term[cite: 774].
* This benefit ceases on the death of the life assured[cite: 776].
* The payout frequency can be **Annual** or **Monthly**[cite: 774].
* The GI payout is a percentage of the Sum Assured and is determined by the annual premium amount (premium size)[cite: 777, 778].

**Guaranteed Income Rates (% of Sum Assured)**

| Premium Size | Annual GI Payout | Monthly GI Payout | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lower Premium Band** | 11.00% p.a. | 0.85% p.m. | [cite: 779] |
| **Medium Premium Band** | 12.00% p.a. | 0.90% p.m. | [cite: 779] |
| **Highest Premium Band** | 13.00% p.a. | 1.00% p.m. | [cite: 779] |

*Note: The specific premium amount (e.g., Rs. 48,000) that defines these bands varies depending on the chosen PPT and Policy Term combination[cite: 779].*

### B. Maturity Benefit

* On the life assured's survival to the end of the policy term, the **Sum Assured** is paid[cite: 781].
* This lump sum is paid along with the final instalment of the Guaranteed Income[cite: 781].

### C. Guaranteed Death Benefit (GDB)

* A GDB is payable upon the death of the life assured at any point during the entire policy term[cite: 801].
* The GDB amount is the **highest** of the following:
    * 10 times the annualized premium [cite: 800]
    * The Sum Assured [cite: 800]
    * 105% of the total premiums paid (less any unpaid due premiums) [cite: 800]
* The GDB is **not** reduced by any Guaranteed Income amounts that have already been paid[cite: 801].

**GDB Payout Options**
The policyholder can choose how the death benefit is paid:

* **Option A: Lump sum:** The entire Guaranteed Death Benefit is paid as a single lump sum[cite: 803, 804].
* **Option B: Family Income Payout (FIP):** An amount equal to **110% of the GDB** is paid out in 60 equal monthly installments[cite: 806].

## 7. Policy Administration

* **Policy Loan:**
    * The policy is eligible for a loan if it has acquired a Surrender Value[cite: 810].
    * The minimum loan amount is Rs. 1,000[cite: 811].
    * The maximum loan amount will not exceed **80% of the available Special Surrender Value**[cite: 811].
    * The policy loan interest rate for the 2023-2024 financial year is **9.5%**[cite: 819].

* **Surrender:**
    * The policy acquires a Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV) provided at least **two full years' premiums** have been paid[cite: 848, 851].
    * The amount paid on surrender is the **higher** of the Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV) and the Special Surrender Value (SSV)[cite: 849].
    * The SSV is declared by the company from time to time and is not guaranteed[cite: 857].

* **Reduced Paid-up (RPU):**
    * If at least two full years' premiums are paid and future premiums are discontinued, the policy will acquire a Reduced Paid-up status[cite: 822].
    * The Death Benefit, Maturity Benefit, and Survival Benefits are all reduced[cite: 824, 825, 826].
    * The benefits are multiplied by a factor of (Number of premiums paid / Total number of premiums payable)[cite: 824, 826].
    * The Reduced Paid-up Survival Benefit is *also* multiplied by a "Paid-up value factor" (50% for 2-4 premiums paid, 75% for 5-7 premiums paid, and 100% for 8 or more premiums paid)[cite: 826, 827, 828].

* **Revival:**
    * A policy in a lapsed or RPU state can be revived within a period of **5 years** from the date of the first unpaid premium[cite: 837].
    * Revival requires payment of all due premiums plus late fees (interest)[cite: 839]. The revival interest rate for FY 2023-24 is 9.5%[cite: 845].

* **Grace Period:**
    * A grace period is allowed for premium payment, during which the policy remains in force[cite: 831].
    * **15 days** for the monthly premium payment mode[cite: 832].
    * **30 days** for all other modes (Annual, Half-yearly, Quarterly)[cite: 833].

* **Lapse:**
    * If premiums are not paid for at least 2 consecutive years, the policy will lapse at the end of the grace period, and all benefits will cease[cite: 835].

## 8. Key Definitions

* **Annualized Premium:** The premium amount payable in a year, excluding taxes, rider premiums, underwriting extra premiums, and loadings for modal premiums[cite: 807].
* **Total Premiums Paid:** The total of all premiums received, excluding any extra premium, rider premium, and taxes[cite: 808].

## 9. Key Terms & Exclusions

* **Free Look Period:**
    * The policyholder has **15 days** from the date of receiving the policy document to review the terms and conditions[cite: 864].
    * If the policy is sourced through **Distance Marketing**, this period is extended to **30 days**[cite: 864].

* **Suicide Exclusion:**
    * If the life assured dies by suicide within **12 months** from the date of risk commencement or the date of revival, the death benefit will not be paid[cite: 869].
    * Instead, the nominee will be entitled to at least **80% of the total premiums paid** or the **surrender value** as of the date of death, whichever is higher, provided the policy is in force[cite: 869].

* **Risk Commencement (Lien):**
    * For lives aged 12 years or older, the risk cover (full death benefit) commences immediately from the policy's start date[cite: 763, 859].
    * For lives **younger than 12 years**, the risk cover commences from the **last day of the 2nd policy year**[cite: 764, 860].
    * If death occurs *before* this risk commencement date, only the total premiums paid (without interest) will be refunded[cite: 766, 862].
