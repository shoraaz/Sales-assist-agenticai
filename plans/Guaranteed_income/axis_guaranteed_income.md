## 4. Plan Variants Breakdown

The plan offers 5 distinct variants:

### Variant 1: Wealth for Milestones
* **Benefit:** Provides a lump sum benefit at maturity[cite: 124, 136].
* **Maturity Benefit:** Sum Assured on Maturity + Accrued Guaranteed Additions[cite: 137, 212].
* **Guaranteed Additions:** 12% of Sum Assured on Maturity, accruing in the last 4 policy years[cite: 212].

### Variant 2: Regular Wealth
* **Benefit:** Provides regular income for a fixed period[cite: 124].
* **Income Period:** 5, 10, or 15 years[cite: 138, 169].
* **Survival Benefit:** Income Benefit + Loyalty Income Booster (equal to 20% of Income Benefit)[cite: 187].
* **Maturity Benefit:** Not applicable[cite: 212].

### Variant 3: Long Term Wealth
* **Benefit:** Provides long-term regular income plus a return of premiums at maturity[cite: 124].
* **Income Period:** 20, 25, or 30 years[cite: 140, 169].
* **Survival Benefit:** Income Benefit + Loyalty Income Booster (equal to 40% of Income Benefit)[cite: 187].
* **Maturity Benefit:** Sum Assured on Maturity (equal to Total Premiums Payable)[cite: 142, 212].

### Variant 4: Early Wealth
* **Benefit:** Provides regular income (can start from 1st year) plus a lump sum at maturity[cite: 124, 143, 144].
* **Survival Benefit:** Income Benefit[cite: 187].
* **Maturity Benefit:** Sum Assured on Maturity + Accrued Guaranteed Additions[cite: 145, 212].
* **Guaranteed Additions:** 25% of Sum Assured on Maturity, accruing in the last 4 years of the PPT[cite: 212].

### Variant 5: Lifelong Wealth
* **Benefit:** Provides income up to age 100, a money-back payout at age 85, and a return of premiums at maturity[cite: 124, 148].
* **Policy Term:** (100 - Entry Age) years[cite: 147].
* **Survival Benefit:** Income Benefit + Loyalty Income Booster (equal to 40% of Income Benefit)[cite: 187].
* **Money Back Benefit:** 50% of Total Premiums Payable, paid at age 85[cite: 149, 187].
* **Maturity Benefit:** 100% of Total Premiums Payable[cite: 150, 212].

## 5. Benefit Details

### A. Death Benefit
The death benefit depends on whether the Policy Continuance Benefit (PCB) is opted for[cite: 216].

**1. When PCB is NOT Opted:** [cite: 219]
The Death Benefit is the highest of:
* Sum Assured on Death [cite: 221]
* 105% of (Total Premiums Paid + underwriting extras + modal loadings) [cite: 221]
* 125% of Surrender Value as on date of death [cite: 221, 226]
(For Single Pay, it's the higher of Sum Assured on Death or 125% of Surrender Value [cite: 221])

**2. When PCB is Opted:** [cite: 230]
* **Immediate Payout:** A lump sum Death Benefit equal to the higher of: [cite: 232]
    * Sum Assured on Death [cite: 233]
    * 105% of (Total Premiums Paid + underwriting extras + modal loadings) [cite: 234]
* **Policy Continuance:** The policy continues without any future premium payments. All future Survival Benefits and the Maturity Benefit are paid to the beneficiary as and when due[cite: 235].

**3. Additional Accidental Death Benefit:** [cite: 238]
* An additional amount equal to **50% of the Sum Assured on Death** is payable[cite: 240, 575].
* This is an inbuilt benefit for non-single premium variants[cite: 238].
* It is payable if death by accident occurs *after* the premium payment term and before the end of the policy term (or age 80, whichever is earlier)[cite: 44, 569, 87].

### B. Other Benefits

* **Loyalty Income Boosters:** Payable during the income period for Variants 2 (20% of income), 3 (40% of income), and 5 (40% of income)[cite: 187]. These are paid only if all premiums have been paid[cite: 193].
* **Guaranteed Additions (GA):** Accrue under Variants 1 and 4 during the last four years of the policy term or PPT, respectively, and are paid at maturity[cite: 212].
* **Money Back Benefit:** Applicable only to Variant 5. A lump sum equal to 50% of Total Premiums Payable is paid when the life insured attains 85 years of age[cite: 187, 149, 197].

## 6. Optional Features & Riders

### A. Core Plan Options
* **Policy Continuance Benefit (PCB):** Available for Variants 1, 2, 3, and 4 (non-single pay)[cite: 29, 173]. If opted, on the death of the Life Insured, future premiums are waived, and the policy benefits (survival/maturity) continue to be paid to the beneficiary[cite: 174].
* **"Save the Date" Option:** (Variants 2, 3, 4, 5) [cite: 48, 444] Allows the policyholder to receive their annual income payout on any chosen special date[cite: 40]. This is only available for annual frequency and arrear payouts[cite: 445].
* **Income Accrual Option:** (Variants 2, 3, 4, 5) [cite: 469] The policyholder can choose to not receive the income benefits as they fall due and instead accumulate them with the company[cite: 41, 469]. The accrued amount will earn interest at the prevailing RBI Repo Rate[cite: 473].
* **Settlement Options:** The policyholder or beneficiary can choose to receive the **Death Benefit** [cite: 484] or **Maturity Benefit** [cite: 497] in installments instead of a lump sum. Options include:
    * Regular Monthly Income for 60 months [cite: 486, 498]
    * Regular Annual Income for 5 years [cite: 490, 499]

### B. Optional Riders
The following riders can be added for an additional premium[cite: 26, 102]:
* Axis Max Life Waiver of Premium plus Rider [cite: 102, 539]
* Axis Max Life Accidental Death and Dismemberment Rider [cite: 102, 541]
* Axis Max Life Term plus Rider [cite: 102, 546]
* Axis Max Life Critical Illness and Disability Rider [cite: 102, 547]
*(Note: No riders are available for policies sold through POS persons)* [cite: 102, 548]

## 7. Policy Administration

* **Policy Loan:** A loan can be availed up to 80% of the policy's surrender value[cite: 623], provided the policy has acquired a surrender value. The minimum loan amount is Rs. 10,000[cite: 625].
* **Surrender:**
    * **Single Pay:** Acquires surrender value immediately[cite: 284, 287].
    * **Other than Single Pay:** Acquires Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV) after payment of two full years' premium[cite: 285].
* **Revival:** A lapsed policy can be revived within a **5-year period** from the due date of the first unpaid premium[cite: 513]. Revival requires payment of all overdue premiums plus a late fee[cite: 514].
* **Grace Period:**
    * **30 days** for Annual, Semi-annual, and Quarterly modes[cite: 616].
    * **15 days** for Monthly mode[cite: 616].

## 8. Key Definitions

* **Annualised Premium:** The premium payable in one year, excluding taxes, rider premiums, and underwriting extra premiums[cite: 559].
* **Sum Assured on Death:** [cite: 563]
    * **For Single Pay:** 1.25 or 10 times the Single Premium (as chosen by policyholder)[cite: 566].
    * **For Other than Single Pay:** 11 times the Annualized Premium[cite: 568].
* **Sum Assured on Maturity:** The guaranteed absolute amount payable at maturity, as specified by the chosen variant[cite: 562].
* **Accident:** A sudden, unforeseen, and involuntary event caused by external, visible, and violent means[cite: 577].

## 9. Key Terms & Exclusions

* **Free Look Period:** The policyholder has **30 days** from the date of receiving the policy document to review the terms and cancel the policy if desired[cite: 585].
* **Suicide Exclusion:** If the Life Insured dies by suicide within **12 months** from the policy's inception or revival date, the death benefit is not paid. Instead, the higher of the Surrender Value or (Total Premiums Paid + loadings) will be paid[cite: 598, 600, 602].
* **Additional Accidental Death Benefit Exclusions:** No benefit is paid if death is caused by factors such as:
    * Intentional self-inflicted injury or suicide [cite: 605]
    * Influence of drugs, alcohol, or narcotics [cite: 606]
    * War, invasion, or hostilities [cite: 607]
    * Participation in criminal acts [cite: 610]
    * Engaging in hazardous pursuits or professional sports [cite: 612]
* **Waiting Period (POSP Only):** For policies sold by Point of Sale Persons (POSP), a **90-day waiting period** applies from the date of risk acceptance[cite: 241, 589]. If death (other than by accident) occurs during this period, 100% of premiums paid will be refunded[cite: 242, 590, 594]. This waiting period does not apply to accidental death[cite: 243, 595].
