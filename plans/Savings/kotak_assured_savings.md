# Kotak Assured Savings Plan

**Plan Type:** A Non-Participating Non-Linked Life Insurance Individual Savings Product [cite: 609]

---

## Key Advantages

* **Guaranteed Maturity Benefit:** Payable at maturity, consisting of the Basic Sum Assured (BSA) + Accrued Guaranteed Yearly Additions (GYA) + Guaranteed Loyalty Addition (GLA)[cite: 628, 629, 630].
* **Increasing Life Cover:** Guaranteed Yearly Additions are added each time a premium is paid, which are payable at maturity or on earlier death[cite: 631].
* **Higher Benefits for Longer Commitment:** Guaranteed Yearly Additions and Guaranteed Loyalty Addition increase with a longer premium payment term (PPT)[cite: 631].
* **High Premium Benefit:** Offers more value (higher Basic Sum Assured) for higher premiums[cite: 632].
* **Rider Options:** Ability to enhance protection with a wide range of optional riders[cite: 633].
* **Tax Benefits:** May be available under u/s 80(C) for premiums paid and u/s 10(10D) for benefits received, as per tax laws[cite: 633].

---

## How the Plan Works

1.  **Pay Premiums:** You pay premiums for the selected premium payment term (e.g., 5, 6, 7, or 10 years)[cite: 635, 693].
2.  **Policy Continues:** After the premium payment term ends, the policy continues to be in force until the maturity date[cite: 636].
3.  **Receive Maturity Benefit:** On survival to the end of the policy term, you receive the Guaranteed Maturity Benefit. This includes:
    * Basic Sum Assured (BSA) [cite: 637, 684]
    * Accrued Guaranteed Yearly Additions (GYA) [cite: 637, 685]
    * Guaranteed Loyalty Addition (GLA) [cite: 637, 685]

---

## Benefits in Detail

### Guaranteed Yearly Additions (GYA)

* GYA is calculated as a percentage of the Cumulative Annualized Premium paid each year[cite: 690].
* It accrues during the premium payment term and is paid out at maturity or on earlier death[cite: 691].
* The rate depends on the chosen PPT[cite: 692]:
    * **5 years PPT:** 7% [cite: 693]
    * **6 years PPT:** 8% [cite: 693]
    * **7 years PPT:** 9% [cite: 693]
    * **10 years PPT:** 10% [cite: 693]

### Guaranteed Loyalty Addition (GLA)

* GLA is calculated as a percentage of the Basic Sum Assured and is paid only at maturity[cite: 696].
* The rate depends on the chosen PPT[cite: 697]:
    * **5 years PPT:** 10% [cite: 698]
    * **6 years PPT:** 12% [cite: 698]
    * **7 years PPT:** 14% [cite: 698]
    * **10 years PPT:** 20% [cite: 698]

### Death Benefit

In the event of the life insured's death during the policy term, the nominee receives the **higher of (a) or (b)**[cite: 664]:

a.  **Basic Death Benefit** + **Guaranteed Yearly Additions** accrued as on the date of death[cite: 665].
b.  **105% of all premiums paid** (excluding extra premium, if any) till the date of death[cite: 666].

* **Basic Death Benefit is defined as:**
    * **For entry age < 50 years:** Higher of 11 times annualized premium or the Guaranteed minimum death benefit[cite: 671, 672].
    * **For entry age >= 50 years:** Higher of 7 times annualized premium or the Guaranteed minimum death benefit[cite: 673, 674].
* **Guaranteed minimum death benefit** is a percentage of the Basic Sum Assured, based on the PPT (e.g., 110% for 5 years PPT, 120% for 10 years PPT)[cite: 675, 676].
* **Waiting Period:**
    * Applies only to policies issued through the **POS Channel**[cite: 678].
    * A **90-day waiting period** from the date of commencement of risk applies[cite: 678].
    * If death occurs during this period (other than by accident), 100% of premiums paid (excluding taxes) will be refunded, and the Death Benefit will not be paid[cite: 679, 680].
    * The waiting period does **not** apply to death due to an accident[cite: 680].

### Maturity Benefit

On survival till the end of the policy term, the Guaranteed Maturity Benefit is paid, which is the sum of[cite: 682, 683]:
* Basic Sum Assured [cite: 684]
* Accrued Guaranteed Yearly Additions [cite: 685]
* Guaranteed Loyalty Addition [cite: 685]

## Other Features & Conditions

### Additional Protection (Riders)

You can attach optional riders to the base plan[cite: 708]:
* Kotak Term Benefit Rider (KTB) [cite: 708]
* Kotak Accidental Death Benefit Rider (ADB) [cite: 708]
* Kotak Permanent Disability Benefit Rider (PDB) [cite: 709]
* Kotak Life Guardian Benefit Rider (LGB) [cite: 714]
* Kotak Accidental Disability Guardian Benefit Rider (ADGB) [cite: 715]
* Kotak Critical Illness Plus Benefit Rider (CIP) [cite: 716]
* **Note:** Riders are **not allowed** for policies availed through the POS Channel[cite: 714, 760].

### Policy Loan

* You can avail loans up to **50% of the Surrender Value**[cite: 714, 762].
* The minimum loan amount is ₹10,000[cite: 714].
* The current interest rate is 8.95% p.a. (subject to revision) [cite: 764].

### Reduced Paid-Up Benefit

* If the policy has acquired a Surrender Value (after at least one full year's premiums are paid [cite: 803]) and subsequent premiums are not paid within the grace period, the policy converts to a Reduced Paid-Up policy[cite: 714, 803].
* A Reduced Paid-Up policy is **not eligible** for future Guaranteed Yearly Additions or the Guaranteed Loyalty Addition[cite: 806].
* The maturity and death benefits are reduced proportionally based on the premiums paid versus total premiums payable[cite: 811, 814].

### Surrender

* The policy acquires a Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV) provided all due premiums for at least **2 policy years** have been paid in full[cite: 781].
* A Special Surrender Value (SSV) is payable after completion of the first year, provided one full year's premium has been received[cite: 795].
* The Surrender Value paid will be the **higher of GSV or SSV**[cite: 780].

### Grace Period

* **30 days** for yearly, half-yearly, and quarterly modes[cite: 757].
* **15 days** for the monthly mode[cite: 757].

### Lapse

* If at least one full year's premium is **not** paid, the policy shall lapse at the end of the grace period, and no benefits will be payable[cite: 769].

### Policy Revival

* A lapsed or Reduced Paid-Up policy can be revived within **five years** of the first unpaid premium or before the end of the policy term, whichever is earlier[cite: 774].
* Revival requires payment of outstanding premiums with revival charges (currently 9% p.a.)[cite: 775, 776].
* Evidence of good health may be required if revival is done after six months from the date of the first unpaid premium[cite: 776].

### Free Look Period

* You have **30 days** from the date of receiving the policy document to review the terms and conditions[cite: 828].
* If you are not agreeable, you can return the policy for cancellation and receive a refund of the premium paid, subject to deductions for proportionate risk premium, stamp duty, and medical expenses (if any)[cite: 830, 835].

### General Exclusion (Suicide)

* If the life insured commits suicide within **12 months** of the date of commencement of risk, 80% of the total premiums paid will be payable[cite: 839].
* If suicide occurs within 12 months of revival (when revival is done *after* 6 months from the first unpaid premium), the benefit will be the higher of 80% of total premiums paid or the Surrender Value[cite: 841].
